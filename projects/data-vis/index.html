Tracking My Musical Progress

from November 24, 2021 to December 2, 2021

About

This visualization is a personal log of my cello practice sessions for 8 days. With it, I seek to capture my progress as a cellist as well as how over time, a piece can become more familiar and personalized to one's playing style.

Terms

  • Staff: a set of five horizontal lines and four spaces that each represent a different musical pitch
  • Measure: A single unit of time, designated by vertical measure lines that run perpendicular to the staff
  • Note: a symbol denoting a the pitch and duration of a musical sound
  • Rest: a symbol that indicates the absence of a sound

Key

  • Each measure represents a single practice session, for a total of 8 practice sessions. Within each measure, there are 5 notes that represent the 5 main sections of my cello practice: scales, etudes (exercises), solo #1, solo #2, and solo #3.
  • The height of the note on the staff represents how happy I am with my progress for the section--the higher the note (vertically & pitch-wise) the note is on the staff, the happier I am.
  • The type of note represents the length of time spent for a given section. The eighth note represents 5-10 minutes. The quarter note represents 10-15 minutes. The half note represents 15-30 minutes.
  • A rest represents a physical rest that I took in the middle of the practice session
